Help - Home
Image Resolution

Use the drop-down list to select the desired video size. Can be Auto, 640 x 480, 320 x 240 or 160 x 120.

Zoom Camera 		Click zoom and then use your mouse to select the section you want to magnify. Click the icon again to increase the zoom to x4  from x2.

Snap Shot 		Use to take a single JPEG or Bitmap picture snapshot of the video image and save it to a desired location on your computer.

Speaker On

Speaker Off 		When the speaker is enabled an icon is displayed on the Home page. You can then use the icons to turn the speaker on and off.

Audio On

Audio Off 		When audio is enabled, an icon is displayed on the Home page. You can then use the icons to turn audio On and Off.

Streaming Mode 		Set the video format to either MPEG-4 or MJPEG.

Ouput Port 1 ON

Ouput Port 1 OFF 		Click on output port 1 icon to turn On and Off.

Ouput Port 2 ON

Ouput Port 2 OFF		 Click on output port 2 icon to turn On and Off.

Day Mode		 Uses the standard filter for day output.

Night Mode 		Uses a filter for night vision.

Start Instant Recording

Click the black icon to start recording, and click the red icon to stop recording. Consider the following:
• The default location for saved recordings is ..\My Documents\Record.
• Click Browse to choose another location to save your recordings.
• Your folder selection is remembered in the registry. If that folder no longer exists the next time the camera is used, the default location is restored.
• The file name is saved in .avi format by default, and the maximum length for the video file is 30 minutes. If the recording is longer than 30 minutes, a second file is created automatically.

• The available disk space required is at least 750 MB.

Stop Instant Recording 		Click this button to stop the instant recording.
Preset Camera View Select the preset area to patrol.

Camera Patrol 		Moves the camera in order one time through the Preset Positions.

Move Control

Allows you to incrementally adjust the camera in 4 directions, and back to home. Please note that there may be a short delay after a direction is clicked

Note: The Move Control becomes visible upon enabling RS-485. You also need to connect the camera to an external Pelco D Protocol device for the Move Control to work.
